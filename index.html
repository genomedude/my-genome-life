Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How early can you test for fetal gender?
We can collect and test as early as 8 weeks gestation — at least 6 weeks before any ultrasound can even attempt to determine sex. We isolate actual cell-free fetal DNA, not camera images.
Why is at-home DIY testing risky?
DIY kits are extremely susceptible to male DNA contamination — from partners, sons, male pets, or even airborne skin cells. Because 51% of live births are naturally male, contamination looks "right" about half the time. Over 98% of documented retail errors are false boy results caused by home contamination.
What raw DNA files do you accept for audits?
We analyze raw data text files downloaded directly from AncestryDNA, 23andMe, or MyHeritage. Each file contains over 600,000 specific genetic lines that we manually cross-reference — no automated AI software involved.
Are the raw data audits medical advice?
No. Our audits are strictly informational and educational. We provide variant tracking and lifestyle workaround insights — not medical diagnoses or treatment protocols. We recommend discussing findings with a qualified healthcare provider.
How does the sealed envelope reveal work?
When the lab results arrive, we deliver or mail the final report inside a strictly sealed, opaque envelope to your chosen recipient. Your baby's gender remains completely private until the moment you break the seal at your reveal event.
What areas do you currently serve?
We serve the Houston–Galveston metropolitan area for mobile in-home collections. Raw data audits can be submitted remotely from anywhere. Contact us for service-area specifics.
